UAE's e-Invoicing Mandate Creates a B2B Payments Opportunity

The UAE e-Invoicing mandate rolls out in two waves starting 2027. It digitizes invoices -- but not payments. For Visa and Mashreq, this gap is greenfield.

Jul 2026

Wave 1 -- Large Businesses

Pilot starts Jul 2026 for businesses with revenue >= AED 50M. Mandatory from Jan 2027.

Jul 2027

Wave 2 -- SMEs

All remaining businesses (revenue < AED 50M) go live. ASP appointment by Mar 2027.

100%

B2B Coverage

All VAT-registered businesses issuing B2B and B2G invoices must comply

The Mandate Solves Invoicing. Not Payments.

What e-Invoicing Solves

  • Invoice creation -- structured PINT AE / UBL 2.1 format, auto-validated
  • Invoice delivery -- Peppol network, guaranteed receipt, audit trail
  • Invoice verification -- auto-ingested, schema-validated, de-duplicated

What It Doesn't Solve

Payment collection remains disconnected.

No mechanism to embed payment links, trigger payments, or reconcile invoices with payment status. Manual bank transfers, cheques, 30-90 day DSO.

This is the gap Visa and Mashreq fill.

"Every e-Invoice carries a Visa payment link. Every payment routes through Visa rails. Every validated invoice feeds Mashreq's financing pipeline."

V

For Visa

Capture SME payment volume at the point of invoice creation. SMEs lack treasury infrastructure -- embedded Visa payment links in every e-Invoice give them a one-click payment path, making Visa the default B2B rail.

M

For Mashreq

Finance against pre-validated, FTA-compliant invoice data. Every invoice passing the platform is schema-validated, de-duplicated, and government-reported -- a pre-qualified lending asset at scale.

What Visa and Mashreq Capture

This partnership turns a compliance mandate into compounding payment volume for Visa and a new financing pipeline for Mashreq.

V

B2B Payment Volume

  • Every e-Invoice becomes a Visa transaction -- payment links embedded at the point of invoice creation, not as an afterthought
  • All VAT-registered businesses -- mandatory compliance drives the volume, Visa captures the payment layer
  • Displace cheques and bank transfers -- digital-first B2B payments, increasing Visa's share of UAE commercial transactions
  • Rich B2B payment data -- invoice amounts, payment timing, industry patterns, buyer-seller relationships
M

  • Finance against FTA-validated invoices -- every invoice is schema-checked, de-duplicated, and government-reported before reaching Mashreq
  • New SME lending channel -- every VAT-registered business entering the e-Invoicing pipeline is a potential financing customer
  • Reduced underwriting risk -- pre-validated, de-duplicated data with government clearance UUID as proof of legitimacy
  • Supply chain financing -- pay sellers immediately via Visa rails, collect from buyers on terms, earning the spread
